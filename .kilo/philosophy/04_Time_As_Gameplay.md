# 04 - Time As Gameplay

## Principle

Time is a gameplay resource.

Timers are valid when they create planning, anticipation, risk, or meaningful pacing.

## Good Timer Use

A timer is good when it asks the player to think about:

- opportunity cost
- team assignment
- risk level
- preparation
- rewards
- long-term scheduling
- offline progress

## Weak Timer Use

A timer is weak when it only delays progress without adding strategy.

Avoid timers that exist only as artificial friction.

## Examples

Good timer systems:

- dungeon expedition
- crafting
- research
- training
- construction
- travel
- settlement upgrade
- guild mission

## AI Rule

When adding a timer, also define why the duration matters and what decision it creates for the player.
